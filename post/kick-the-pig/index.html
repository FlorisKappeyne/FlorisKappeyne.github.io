Kick the Pig

A 2D PC game with custom physics where you place objects that influence the flight of a pig towards a goal.

For Kick the Pig is a project with custom 2D physics, multiple levels and the most complete game I made in the first year.

Engine: Unity 5
Tools: P4V, Pivotal Tracker, Visual Studio 2017
Roles: Tools programmer, UI programmer, Gameplay programmer
Team: 19 people (7 programmers, 6 designers, 7 artists)
Lifetime: IGAD Year 1 block D, 2016, 8 weeks


The project

Kick the Pig is a physics based game where you put a dirty pig into a bath tub by kicking him into the air and guide his trajectory using magnets and repulsors until the pig is finally clean. All physics in this game are custom made.

Click here to download the game. Simply run the executable and you’re good to go.

What I did

  • Physics programming (both collision detection and resolution), with moving and static circles, and static polygons. See the video below for a demonstration. Note that even though the video doesn’t show it, the framerate was above 60 fps at all times.
  • Gameplay programming (the catapult, placing and moving the gameplay objects, a bit of game flow)
